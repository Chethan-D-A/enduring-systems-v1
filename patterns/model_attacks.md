# Model Attacks

## Purpose
This document records the strongest known attacks against **Draft v1.1** of the model.

The objective is not to defend the model but to identify its weaknesses. If the model survives these attacks, confidence in it increases. If it fails, the model must be revised.

---

### Attack 1: Necessity Attack
The model assumes several mechanisms are important for long-term persistence.
* **Question:** Are these mechanisms actually necessary?
* **Examples:**
  * **Zoroastrianism** survived despite becoming relatively small and resource-poor.
  * Some **family businesses** survive despite losing large amounts of founder knowledge.
* **Possible Implication:** A mechanism may be helpful without being necessary.

### Attack 2: Sufficiency Attack
The model identifies several mechanisms that appear repeatedly.
* **Question:** Is any single mechanism sufficient by itself?
* **Examples:**
  * **Nalanda** possessed significant knowledge continuity, yet the institution disappeared.
  * The **Dutch East India Company** possessed extensive structures and governance systems, yet collapsed.
* **Possible Implication:** No individual mechanism may be sufficient. Persistence may require multiple mechanisms working together.

### Attack 3: Hidden Variable Attack
The proposed mechanisms may not be independent.
* **Question:** Are multiple mechanisms merely symptoms of deeper mechanisms?
* **Examples:**
  * *Knowledge Continuity*, *Identity Continuity*, and *Renewal Mechanisms* may all depend on a deeper variable such as **commitment**.
  * *Structural Capacity*, *Succession Capacity*, and *Incentive Alignment* may all depend on **governance quality**.
* **Possible Implication:** The current model may be describing effects rather than root causes.

### Attack 4: Survival vs Endurance Attack
The research question concerns enduring value-generating systems.
* **Question:** Does the model explain survival or does it explain endurance?
* **Examples:**
  * A **company** may survive while no longer generating exceptional value.
  * A **religion** may survive while becoming disconnected from its original purpose.
* **Possible Implication:** Persistence alone may not satisfy the definition of endurance.

### Attack 5: Creation vs Persistence Attack
The model separates creation mechanisms from persistence mechanisms.
* **Question:** Is this distinction valid?
* **Examples:**
  * **Founders** may directly create future persistence through institutional design.
  * **Adaptability** may be both a creation mechanism and a persistence mechanism.
* **Possible Implication:** The boundary between creation and persistence may be artificial.

### Attack 6: Founder Dependency Attack
The model focuses heavily on post-founder persistence.
* **Question:** How much of a system's future survival is determined during the founder's lifetime?
* **Examples:**
  * **Toyota Production System**
  * **Berkshire Hathaway's** philosophy
  * **Christian** institutional structures
* **Possible Implication:** Persistence mechanisms may largely be products of founder decisions.

### Attack 7: Scale Attack
* **Question:** Do the same mechanisms operate at all scales?
* **Examples:**
  * **Zoroastrianism** survived as a small community.
  * **Christianity** survived as a global religion.
* **Possible Implication:** Different scales may require different persistence mechanisms.

### Attack 8: Environment Attack
* **Question:** How much of persistence depends on external conditions rather than internal mechanisms?
* **Examples:**
  * **Indus Valley Civilization**
  * **Ancient Egypt**
  * **Byzantine Empire**
* **Possible Implication:** Environmental stability may be more important than internal design.

### Attack 9: Adaptation Attack
The model treats adaptation as important.
* **Question:** Can systems survive through continuity rather than adaptation?
* **Examples:**
  * **Ancient Egypt**
  * Certain **religious traditions**
* **Possible Implication:** Adaptation may not always be required.

### Attack 10: Selection Bias Attack
The dataset contains only fifteen cases.
* **Question:** Are the observed mechanisms genuine or artifacts of case selection?
* **Possible Implication:** Different case selections may produce different models.

---

## Status
> [!WARNING]
> **None of these attacks are considered resolved.**
> 
> Each attack should be evaluated against the case studies before **Draft v2.0** is produced.
