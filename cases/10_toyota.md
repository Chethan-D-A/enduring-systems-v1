# Toyota

Type: Corporation / Manufacturing Company

Founder: Kiichiro Toyoda

Origin: Japan

Age/Duration: Approximately 88+ years (1937 – Present)

What value it generated: Toyota focused heavily on affordability and reliable transportation. Their manufacturing innovation, employment creation, global supply chains, engineering and production knowledge has made them one of the largest and most valuable companies. They created the Toyota Production System (TPS) and continuous improvement in methodologies and adapted worlwide.

Why it appears to have survived: Toyota appears have survived because of its operational knowledge into systems and processes rather than relying on individual leaders. The Toyota Production System created repeatable methods that could be taught, imporved and transmitted across generations of employees. Its culture of continuous improvement and adaptation allowed it to respond to changing markets and technologies while maintaining a consistent organizational identity.

Questions it raises: 
1. Can processes become more important than leaders for long-term survival?
2. Is codified knowledge more durable than tacit knowledge?
3. How does an organization preserve excellence across generations of employees?
4. Is continuous improvement itself a survival mechanism?
5. What happens when an organization's core processes become outdated?

Possible Mechanisms: 
- Codified knowledge
- Process institutionalization
- Continuous improvement
- Knowledge transfer
- Adaptability
- Distributed expertise
- Operational excellence
