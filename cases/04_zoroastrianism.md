# Zoroastrianism

Type: Religion

Founder: Zarathustra (Zoroaster)

Origin: Ancient Persia

Age/Duration: Roughly 2500 to 3500 years

What value it generated: Zoroastrianism had a moral framework centered around good thoughts, good words and good deeds. It had a shared cultural and religious identity for Persians. Its worldview emphasizes ethical choice and personal responsibility. It has many ancient rituals, texts and traditions preserved across generations. It has had some influence on later religious thoughts and traditions.

Why it has survived: Zoroastrianism has survived (though only a small percentage of what had been in its peak) because of its strong community identity and families preserving its identity, traditions, rituals and culture. Even after losing political power and state support, it has continued through cultural continuity and intergenerational transfer of knowledge and tradition despite rapidly shrinking in size and now becoming one of the minor religions.

Questions it rises:
1. How does a system survive even after losing political and state support while being suppressed?
2. Can a small but highly committed community outperform a large but weakly committed one?
3. Is survival easier through institutions or through community identity?

Possible Mechanisms:
- Strong community identity
- Intergenerational transfer of knowledge and traditions
- Cultural continuity
- Adaptation to minority status
- Survival without dependence on political/state power
