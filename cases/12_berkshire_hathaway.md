# Berkshire Hathaway

Type: Holding Company / Conglomerate

Founder: Originally founded as a textile company, transformed into its modern day form by Warren Buffet and Charlie Munger

Origin: United States

Age/Duration: Original company traces back to 1839, Modern Berkshire Hathaway era began in 1965.

What value it generated: Berkshire Hathaway originally a textile company transformed into a holding company, demonstates long term capital allocation and preservation of growth of shareholder wealth. It shows aquisition and strewardship of business, decentralised management of subsidiaries and investment knowledge and business education. They have had a stable ownership structures for acquired companies and efficient deployment of capital across industries.

Why it appears to have survived: Berkshire Hathaway appears to have survived and prospered because it built a system around principles rather than day-to-day founder intervention. Its decentralized structure allows subsidiaries to operate independently while capital allocation remains centralized. Buffett and Munger spent decades codifying their philosophy through annual letters, shareholder meetings, and organizational culture, creating a framework that can potentially survive beyond them.
