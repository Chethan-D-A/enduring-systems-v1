# Buddhism

Type: Religion

Founder: Siddartha Gautama (The Buddha/ Gautama Buddha)

Origin: India

Age/Duration: Roughly 2500 years

Values it generated: Buddhism revolves around peace and suffering. It has offered a systemic framework for understanding suffering through practical methods like meditation and mindfulness. It has a strict ethical guidelines for individual conduct and has monastic communities that have constantly preserved its teachings and preachings. It has a flexible philosophy that could integrate into different cultures and large bodies of it are philosophical and psychological insights.

Why it has survived: Buddhism has survived because it created institutions capable of preserving teachings while also allowing significant adaptation across cultures. Unlike many systems tied to specific state or ethnicity, Buddhism has spread from ancient India into China, Japan, Tibet and other south-east regions. Its peak spread happened during the period of Mauryan Empire.

Questions it rises:
1. How can a system remain recognizable while constantly adapting to many cultures?
2. Is flexibility is a system more important for survival than strict preservation?
3. Can a system survive is its origin place declines?

Possible Mechanisms:
- Adaptability across cultures
- Codification of teachings into texts
- Preservation through monastic institutions
- Practical benefits experienced by followers
- Ability to coexist with locan traditions
- geographic diversification
