# Mauryan Empire

Type: Empire / State

Founder: Chandragupta Maurya

Origin: Ancient India

Age/Duration: Approximately 137 years (c. 322 BCE – 185 BCE)

What value it generated: Mauryan Empire was one of the most early Ancient Indian empires which unified almost entire indian subcontinents. They had one of the most sophisticated administrative and governance systems. They demonstrated great internal stability and secirity across vast territories. They had developed one of the earliest vast empire the subcontinent had not seen in centuries. The state capacity was unprecedented for their time. They played an important role in spread of ideas, culture and administration. Their trade and economic reforms were renowned and also had an early constitution like book called "Arthashastra" written by Chanakya (Kautilya).

Why it appears failed: The Mauryan Empire appears to have struggled a lot after the peace transformation started by its third ruler, Emperor Ashoka the Great, after he chose Buddhism. While it had developed a very sophisticated and advanced powerful administrative structures, it seemed to be heavily dependent on an exceptional leadership. After apparently three rulers and the peace transformation, later successors though powerful struggled to keep check on provincial governors and a weakened army morale.

Questions it raises:
1. Why do some systems collapse shortly after losing execptional leaders?
2. Is succession more important than growth?
3. What is the minimum institutional strength required for a system to outlive its founder?
4. Why did highly successful empire like it fail to persist even two centuries?

Posssible Mechanisms:
- Dependent on expectional leadership
- Succession quality
- Institutional strength
- Administrative capacity
- Territoral scale
- Adaptation after leadership transitions
