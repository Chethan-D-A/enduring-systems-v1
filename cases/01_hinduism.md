# Hinduism

Type: Decentralized Religion

Founder: No single founder

Origin: India

Age/Duration: Roughly 4000-5000 years old

What value it generated: Hinduism is considered the oldest living religion in the world. With roughly 5000 years of history, it has an immense collection of sacred texts, rituals and practices. It has constantly been evolving by the contribution of Rishis (Sages) and saints and scholars. It has provided the world a wide range of inventions, texts, rituals, practices, healthcare and a lifestyle.

Why it has survived: Despite undergoing thousands of years of invasion, Hinduism has successfully held together mainly due its its decentralised nature and vast belivers. The knowledge was decentralised so well that an invasion in one part could not erase its persistence in the other parts. 

Questions it rises: 
1. How does a decentralised religion without a leader manage to exist for so long even with invasions and still hold significant knowledge and vast belivers?
2. Are all decentralised systems immune to extinction?
3. What made it so different from other decentralised systems?

Possible Mechanisms: 
- Decentralization
- Distributed knowledge storage
- Continuous knowledge pass through families
- Ability to absorb and integrate new ideas
- Large geographic spread
- Vast knowledge and deep rooted culture
- Being the oldest living religion
