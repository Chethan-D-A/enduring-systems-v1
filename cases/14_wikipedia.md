# Wikipedia

Type: Knowledge Institution / Collaborative Information System

Founder: Founded by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger

Origin: Internet

Age/Duration: Approximately 24+ years (2001 – Present)

What value it generated: Wikipedia played an important role in providing global access to knowledge, collaborative knowledge creation and refinement. It played a major role in preservation and organization of information. It provided educational resources in near zero cost. creating a rapid dissemination of knowledge, creation of a shared repository of human information. It demostrated exceptional large scale volunteer coordination.

Why it appears to have survived: Wikipedia appears to have survived because knowledge creation, maintenance, and governance are distributed across a large community rather than concentrated in a few individuals. The platform has developed governance systems, policies, and norms that allow the project to continue despite the departure of individual contributors. Its mission is simple, widely understood, and continuously reinforced by contributors.

Questions it raises:
1. Can a system survive indefinitely if no individual is indispensable?
2. How much decentralization is optimal for long-term survival?
3. How can large groups coordinate without centralized control?
4. Is community ownership stronger than founder ownership?
5. Can digital systems preserve knowledge more effectively than physical institutions?
6. What failure modes exist for highly decentralized systems?

Possible Mechanisms:
- Distributed ownership
- Redundency
- Community governance
- Shared mission
- Global participation
- Adaptability
